Founder · Global Consultant

Meet Tanna Jackson.

Tanna Jackson

Tanna Jackson is the founder of Care South Solutions, a USA-based global consulting company focused on humanitarian needs. Her work is rooted in one clear belief: real care should reach the people who need it most, especially women, children, and underserved families.

The Why

Care has no border.

I do not see a border between the child in my community and the child in Kajiado. Care should travel as far as the need does.

Care South Solutions exists to sponsor foundations and projects that create practical, life-changing support for women and children. The mission goes beyond awareness, it is about guidance, hands-on assistance, and building pathways for people to live with more dignity, support, and purpose. That is why this work matters in Kenya, and why disability care for children remains central to the mission.

Global Consultant

Building bridges that hold weight.

Tanna's work as a global consultant is not symbolic. It's operational. From coordinating partnerships across continents to getting wheelchairs into the hands of children in Kajiado, the focus is always on real outcomes, not headlines.

Her work brings together humanitarian projects, local foundations, and mission-aligned partners so support reaches women, children, and families in practical ways. Care South Solutions helps connect vision to execution, from sponsorship support to strategic guidance and community-centered collaboration.

The Company

What Care South Solutions is, and isn't.

We are a global consulting company based in the USA, focusing on humanitarian needs. Our mission includes sponsoring foundations and projects that support women and children, while also providing guidance and assistance to people in need. Care South Solutions exists to improve lives and help people move toward a more purposeful lifestyle through real action, real partnership, and real care.

What we are

  • A global consulting and philanthropy brand
  • Rooted in action and real outcomes
  • Partnered with the Jihan Shariff Foundation in Kenya
  • Focused on disabled and underserved children
  • Driven by one person's conviction, Tanna Jackson

What we aren't

  • A large nonprofit with layers of overhead
  • Symbolic, corporate, or distant
  • Here for the short term
  • Built on anyone else's name
